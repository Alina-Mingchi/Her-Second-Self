### Challenge 05 Hack-Nation

Foundation Models for Women's Hormonal Health 
-- Building the AI Infrastructure for the Next Generation of Women's Health

### Why this matters?

We would like to tackle the challenge of PMS, short for Premenstrual syndrome. This is the case for more than 20-30% of women between the ages of 15 and 50. PMS causes recurring physical and emotional symptoms before menstruation, such as mood changes, bloating, breast tenderness, and fatigue. Diagnosis requires a long-term symptom diary over several cycles to confirm the cyclical pattern and rule out other causes properly. 

### Overview

I want to build a website that could be used as long term, reproduceble, and robust infrastructure using AI to accelarate female hormonal related resesarch. This platform should be able to collect multimodal data, including hormone measures, wearable data, laboratory data, symptoms, longitudinal physiological signals. Research has shown that hormones shift continuously with sleep, stress, nutition, and age. I would like to make the platform capable also to have the user taken picture of their food, and with AI tools, extract food, and estimate nutrition. From wearable data, extract sleep, stress, heart rate. Since hormone shape brain function, cardiovascular health, and metabolism, I would like to have the possibility of collecting EEG signals, or other brain signals, and let user do a voice memo, from which, we could collect psychological cue and symptom such as migraine, or fatigue, or brain fog.   This web based platform should work both on laptop, and on phone, since researchers should have it on laptop, if there is laboratory data, and brain signals, it should be collected with the laptop, while users should have it easily open as a website on their phone, to record voice memo. Data privacy is taken care of by asking the participants to sign consent forms for each data modality. In addition, raw data stay local, only extracted features are shared. Participants are indicated with PID in the view of research, clinicians, and admin view.
